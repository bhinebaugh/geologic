  2. Phanerozoic ("visible life")
    Contains the eras shown in the graph below.
    1. Paleozoic ("ancient life")
      marine invertebrates
      1. Cambrian

        542 - 488 million years ago

        multicellular life arises? witnessed radiation of new life forms

      2. Ordovician

        488 - 444 million years ago

        first vertebrates: fish

      3. Silurian

        444 - 416 (395) million years ago

        land plants

      4. Devonian

        416 - 359 (345) million years ago

        amphibians, insects

      5. Carboniferous

        359 - 299 (280) million years ago

      6. Permian

        299 - 251 (225) million years ago

        reptiles

    2. Mesozoic ("middle life")
      Gymnosperms (conifers, cycads, ginkgoes)
      1. Triassic

        251 - 200 million years ago

        early dinosaurs, mammal-like reptiles

      2. Jurassic

        200 - 145 million years ago

        flying reptiles, first birds

      3. Cretaceous

        145 - 65 million years ago

        end of dinosaurs

    3. Cenozoic ("recent life")
      The Cenozoic is the most recent geologic era, including the present epoch, the Holocene(?)
      1. Paleogene

        65 - 23 million years ago

        hello mammals. flowering plants (angiosperms) proliferate. subdivided into several Epochs

        1. Paleocene
        2. Eocene
        3. Oligocene
      2. Neogene

        23 - 2.6 million years ago

        More of the same

        1. Miocene
